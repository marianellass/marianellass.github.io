Projects
UConn CS Honors Thesis
I applied Support Vector Regression to investigate whether macroeconomic indicators such as GDP and CPI can improve stock price predictions. Centering on AAPL and comparing it with Walmart and PepsiCo, I found that adding these broader economic factors often reduces predictive accuracy for tech stocks, yet can enhance performance in consumer-driven industries. This thesis highlights the importance of industry context when incorporating macroeconomic signals into AI-based stock forecasting.
Unicorn Startup Analysis Dashboard: Identifying Investment Trends and Educational Background of Founders and Investors
I built an interactive dashboard to uncover patterns in 900+ unicorn companies and 20,000+ startup ecosystem players like founders, CEOs, and VCs. By combining data visualization techniques like treemaps, heatmaps, and network graphs with K-Means and GMM clustering techniques, I identified which countries and industries dominate the unicorn scene and how educational networks—from Universities like Harvard and Stanford—shape the paths of founders and investors. Key insights include the outsize influence of the U.S. and China, the importance of alumni connections in startup success, and notable differences in degree backgrounds between younger founders and more MBA-focused VCs. This project highlights how data-driven insights can help entrepreneurs, investors, and educators better navigate the global venture capital landscape.
Investigating Factors Affecting Success for Underrepresented Minority Students in UConn's School of Engineering
I led an IRB mixed-methods research project investigating the factors affecting success and retention of underrepresented minority students in UConn’s School of Engineering. Through surveys, interviews, and quantitative data analysis, I found that URM students often face challenges such as isolation, cultural mismatch, and foundational knowledge gaps—particularly in introductory STEM courses. Our findings underscore the critical role of social capital and bridging programs like UConn’s BRIDGE in fostering belonging, mitigating high drop/fail/withdraw rates, and ultimately improving retention among these students. This research highlights the importance of inclusive educational environments and proactive measures to ensure URM success in engineering.
TRUMPF Virtual Reality Laser Training
I co-developed a virtual reality training simulation in Unity for the TRUMPF TruLaser 2030 machine. This VR environment gamifies critical tasks—like part rotation, machine setup, and safety checks—so new operators can learn interactively without risking real equipment downtime. By integrating physics-based interactions and guided scenarios, our project offers a hands-on, immersive training solution that improves accessibility, boosts operator confidence, and helps manufacturers streamline their onboarding process.
